Infographics originated in ancient times when people used cave drawings and symbols to convey information.

In the Middle Ages, maps and illustrations in manuscripts became the first examples of data visualization.

In the 19th century, William Playfair invented line graphs, bar charts, and pie charts, laying the foundation for modern infographics

In the 20th century, the development of printing and mass media made infographics a popular tool in journalism

With the advent of computers in the late 20th century, digital technologies accelerated the creation of complex visualizations
